TEST SCORES

What are SAT and ACT Test Scores?

College admission tests, like the SAT and ACT, are standardized tests typically taken in your junior or senior year. Colleges use scores from these tests to help them make admission decisions. Each college has its own admission processes. Here are the facts you need to know. .

1. Majority Four-Year Colleges Use Test Scores in Their Admission Decisions

Colleges use SAT and ACT scores to compare the strengths and readiness of students from different high schools. Keep in mind that scores are just one part of your college application, along with grades, course rigour and recommendations.

2. Test Scores Are Not the Most Important in Your Application

When considering the test scores for admission, different colleges consider the scores differently. Remember that colleges give more importance to your grades and the rigour of your classes not scores.

3. Most Colleges Publish Student Test-Score Information

If you’re interested in a particular college, you can see how your scores compare. Keep in mind that colleges admit students with a wide range of the published scores; there are always some students who score above, or even below. Think of these scores as a guide, not a cut-off. Here is a really good resource compare your scores: College Simple

4. Admission Tests Let Colleges Find You

Once you sign up for the SAT, you get an opportunity to hear from colleges that have an interest in you based on a combination of your test scores, grades, academic interests, and other characteristics.

5. Colleges May Use Scores to Award Scholarships

Most scholarship money comes from colleges. Some colleges use your test scores, alone or in combination with other characteristics and achievements, to award their funds. Some colleges may even automatically award you a scholarship if you earn a certain score.

6. Scores May Use Scores to Determine Placement in College Classes

So some colleges use scores to place students in classes that are at the right level for them. Scores can also be used to identify students who may benefit from specific advisor or academic support in college.

7. Most Four-Year Colleges Require Scores, and Two-Year Colleges Recommend Them

Nearly all four-year colleges require you to submit admission test scores — you are not allowed to apply without them. There is some exceptions like open-admission colleges, including community colleges, don’t require scores but sometimes may use them to give you scholarship and for placement.

8.Who can see your scores?

You are the only one who chooses who can see your scores, because you’re in charge of sending them. If you decide to retake the SAT, you can often pick to send only your best scores. But each college establishes its own policy for how it uses multiple scores

APPLYING

At this point you should have decided on the colleges you want to attend. Now the interesting part is actually applying to said institutions. First, let’s talk about the application process. Colleges can use three different application models: the Common Application, the Universal College Application, and/or their own personal application. You can only choose one to submit to a college, but keep in mind that all are treated equally. Regardless of the model, colleges require basic elements from an applicant: GPA, course difficulty, extracurriculars, and an essay. You can use any of these models to apply Early Decision, Early Action, Early Action-Restricted, or Regular Decision, which are the deadline plans for college admission. Once you receive your decision, you have to make your own. If you were accepted, then you have until May 1st to make your deposit. If you were rejected, then you move on to the next school. If you were waitlisted or deferred, then you can wait for the college’s final decision along with other school’s decisions.

Decision Plans

    Early Decision

Early Decision is a plan that allows a student to apply to a school in the first semester and get a decision back by November 1st. The only drawback is that this decision is binding, the student has to go to this school. Therefore, it is recommended that the student only apply Early Decision to a school if they are certain they want to attend that school.

    Early Action and Early Action-Restricted

Early Action allows students to apply to schools early like in Early Decision except this plan isn't binding, which means student s can apply to as many Early Action plans as they please. Early Action-Restricted, however, limits students to applying to one school until they receive a decision from that school. Thus, it has the application limitations of Early Decision with the freedom of Early Action.

    Regular Decision

Regular Decision is the route that most students take. Students can apply to as many schools as they want in January and receive a non-binding decision by March, at the latest.

FINANCIAL AID

Financial aid is money distributed based on a student's financial need and merit (academic, athletic, etc.). Aid is given to students by the government, colleges, private organizations, or the state in the form of loans, grants, scholarships, and work study programs. The best way to receive these types of aid is through the FAFSA or CSS PROFILE forms.

Based on the guidelines of these forms, a lot of families believe that they don't qualify for aid. Although, approximately 66% of families qualify for some aid. Thus, it is always in your favor to

Types of Financial Aid

    Grants and Scholarship

Grants and scholarships are similar in that they are "free money",they don't have to be paid back, awarded by the government, banks or other federal institutions. Also, it is possible to lose both types of aid due to criminal activity, no longer meeting the requirements, and other factors. The difference lies in the purpose of the two: grants are mostly awarded based on financial need whereas scholarships are based on merit.

    Loans

A student can get a loan from the government. Unlike grants and scholarships, loans have to be paid back with interest, which means students have to pay back more than what they withdrew. Students take use loans more than any other form of financial aid, which tends to leave them in a lot of debt.

    Work Study

The work study program gives students the opportunity to earn money by doing jobs provided by the college. The program is essentially allowing the student to pay off their financial aid. This form of financial aid doesn't cost the student anything but their time. With that in mind, students still have ample time to live their regular lives as it is rare to have a full-time job.

FINAL ACTS

What are my final steps after accepting my college offer?

1.Your college will contact you

Once you accept your college they will contact you with information you need to get ready for the first year. You will need to fill forms for housing preference, registration, orientation and new classes. It’s important to keep up with the deadlines for these events.

2. Make a list of your college goals.

Think about what you what you’d like to accomplish in college, both academically and in terms of your college experience. You can change this list later on, but it is important to enter college with an idea of what your goals are.

3. Find a way to keep in touch with your roommate

It is important to get to know your roommate before you enter college. Find a way to contact and get to know them more whether if it's through email or phone.

4. Connect with your classmates

When you know which college to go to find a way to connect with your classmates, many colleges have social media groups specifically for freshmen. Attend networking events so you can develop friendships with your classmates

5. Look for potential campus activities

Now that you know which college you are attending begin to look for clubs and organizations you are interested in. Being part of these really enhances your college experience. Its an awesome opportunity to network with other students.

6. Save money

It is smart to save money for school early on. Look for summer job opportunities so you have some extra money when you enter school. Apply for part-time job on campus. Apply early so you have a higher chance of getting the job position.
