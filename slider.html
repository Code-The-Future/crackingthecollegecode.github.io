Applying

At this point you should have decided on the colleges you want to attend. Now the interesting part is actually applying to said institutions. First, let’s talk about the application process. Colleges can use three different application models: the Common Application, the Universal College Application, and/or their own personal application. You can only choose one to submit to a college, but keep in mind that all are treated equally. Regardless of the model, colleges require basic elements from an applicant: GPA, course difficulty, extracurriculars, and an essay. You can use any of these models to apply Early Decision, Early Action, Early Action-Restricted, or Regular Decision, which are the deadline plans for college admission. Once you receive your decision, you have to make your own. If you were accepted, then you have until May 1st to make your deposit. If you were rejected, then you move on to the next school. If you were waitlisted or deferred, then you can wait for the college’s final decision along with other school’s decisions.

Financial Aid

Financial aid is money distributed based on a student's financial need and merit (academic, athletic, etc.). Aid is given to students by the government, colleges, private organizations, or the state in the form of loans, grants, scholarships, and work study programs. The best way to receive these types of aid is through the FAFSA or CSS PROFILE forms.
